Langston Hughes

Langston hughes bio

Langston Hughes was born in Joplin Missouri on February 1, 1901. he was raised by his grandma in Kansas until he was 12 years old. He studied at Columbia University where he would later publish his first book of poetry the weary blues. he later went on to publish many other works as he lived and contributed to the Harlem Renaissance.

Significant works

Two of Langston Hughes's most significant works are "The Weary Blues" and "Let America be America Again". Weary blues talks about the music genre that was popularized during the Harlem renaissance, and in "Let America be America Again" Hughes shows his struggles feeling like an outsider that is part of the country.

Historical context

Langston Hughes lived and was part of the Harlem renaissance. it was also a time of heavy segregation and racial injustice. Both of these heavily influenced Langston Hughe's poems. this can be seen in poems like " The weary blues" where he talks about the music that was popularised by the Harlem renaissance like jazz and blues and in "Let America Be America Again" where he talks about racial injustice in the U.S.

Genre and styles

Langston Hughes invented the genre of jazz poetry in which the rhythm of the poem is spoken it sounds like jazz music. Hughes's poems are about African American discrimination. Also, he encourages black people to be proud of their skin color, that one-day black people will be on top and white people feel regret.

Values and beliefs

Langston's, believes that Black people are as good as white people. He believes that black people could do big things and be important people in the world. Also, White people believed that Black people are liars, thieves, and they don't deserver power which he said in his "Remember" poem. Langston also believes that black people are being denied the American dream.
